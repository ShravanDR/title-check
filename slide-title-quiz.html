Slide Title Checklist

Run every title through these before locking it in.

  • Message, not label. Does it state a conclusion an investor could agree or disagree with? If someone can respond "so what?", rewrite it.
  • Works standalone. If someone reads only the titles in sequence, does the story track without the presenter?
  • Leads with the point. No setup, no announcement. Say it.
  • Specific over vague. A name, a number, a company does more work than a category label. "Strong growth" is not a claim. "2.5x users in 5 months" is.
  • Anchored to a norm. A number in isolation carries no weight. State the finding and the reference point it deviates from.
  • Answers "so what?" If the implication for the business is not self-evident, add it. Tell the reader what to do with the information.
  • One point per title. If it's doing two things, the slide isn't ready.
  • No false drama. Cut "here's the thing," "here's the kicker," and any engagement bait. If the point is worth making, make it.
  • No banned patterns. No "not X, it's Y" framing. No participial add-ons. No stacked rule-of-three.
  • Clean language. No delve, synergy, paradigm, tapestry. No filler transitions. No em dashes. No intensifiers that add nothing.
  • Length earns its place. A two-line title that conveys a full finding beats a four-word title that conveys nothing. But padding with qualifiers is not the same as adding information.
  • Social proof names names. "Marquee investors" is empty. "Sequoia, Tiger Global, and DST at $120M" is proof.
